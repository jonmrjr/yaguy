Is our event-driven architecture sane at 1M daily jobs?

Asked: Mar 18, 2025 Answered: Mar 19, 2025 Urgency: Standard

We ingest jobs from multiple partners and push them into a Kafka-based pipeline. We’re about to cross 1M daily jobs and want to know if our current design (Kafka → workers → Postgres) will hold or if we should add another layer.

Attachment: architecture-diagram.pdf

Answer

TL;DR

Yes, but you need to segment Kafka topics and enforce idempotency at the worker layer. Also migrate job result writes to a dedicated Postgres logical replica.

Detailed reasoning

Your load profile is CPU-bound on workers, not Kafka. The bottleneck risk is the shared Postgres cluster doing both job tracking and reporting. Split those concerns and you buy 6–9 months of runway.

Concrete steps

  1. Create separate Kafka topics per partner tier with retention tuned to 48 hours.
  2. Enable dedupe keys at the consumer to enforce exactly-once semantics in your workers.
  3. Replicate the Postgres job_results table to a read-only analytics replica and point dashboards there.

Optional snippets

// Example worker idempotency check
if (hasProcessed(job.id)) {
  return;
}
process(job);
markProcessed(job.id);
